STA 9750 Final Project – Happiness, Work-Life Balance, and Well-Being

1. Motivation

We study how national happiness relates to (i) income, (ii) work–life balance, (iii) weather, and (iv) governance. We focus on describing patterns and do not claim causality.

2. Data Sources

  • World Happiness Report (WHR25 Excel): happiness (life evaluation, 3-year average), explained log GDP per capita, explained perceptions of corruption.
  • OECD Time Use: Leisure minutes/day (LEI) as a work–life balance proxy.
  • World Bank: annual precipitation (AG.LND.PRCP.MM).
Note on “vacation days”: the OECD dataset you provided is a time-use file, not paid vacation days. To stay faithful to the shared data, we use Leisure minutes/day as a vacation-like / work–life balance proxy.

3. Visualizations

3.1 Happiness vs Income

Country-year observations (WHR). Line shows a fitted linear trend.

Finding: Income is strongly associated with happiness; returns typically diminish at higher income levels.

3.2 Happiness vs Leisure (Work–Life Balance Proxy)

OECD leisure minutes/day merged at the country level.

Finding: Leisure time shows a weaker but generally positive association with happiness, consistent with the idea that time off matters more once basic needs are met.

3.3 Happiness vs Precipitation

Weather relationships are often weak at the national level.

Finding: Precipitation is a weaker and less consistent correlate than income or governance measures.

6. Key Takeaways

  • Income is among the strongest correlates of happiness.
  • Work–life balance (leisure proxy) tends to be positively associated with happiness, but is weaker and context-dependent.
  • Weather (precipitation) appears less important in this setup.
  • Corruption perceptions are typically negatively associated with happiness, highlighting the role of trust and institutions.

7. Limitations & Next Steps

  • Correlation does not imply causation; omitted variables may drive patterns.
  • Country averages mask inequality within countries.
  • Swap in a true paid vacation days dataset (if available) to strengthen the vacation analysis.
